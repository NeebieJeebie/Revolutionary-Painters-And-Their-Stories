About Claude Monet

Claude Monet was one of the artists who led the French impressionist movement, he gave the movement its name, his original style is recognizable and some of the most recognizable paintings are Women in the Garden, Water Lilies, Westminster Bridge, etc. Claude Monet was one of the artists who led the French impressionist movement, he gave the movement its name, his original style is recognizable and some of the most recognizable paintings are Women in the Garden, Water Lilies, Westminster Bridge, etc. Claude Monet was born in 1840 in Paris, his father was a successful grocer and his mother died when he was the age of 15, when he was young, he received classes at the College du Havre, as a helper for a famous artist there.

From an early age he created paintings and sold them, taking advantage of his early achieved talent. His life was changed in 1856 when Monet became friends with Eugéne Boudin, a landscape painter famous for his scenes of northern French coastal towns. This friendship taught Monet to paint outdoors and this changed his concept of how art can be created when the subject is changed. In 1859 he was rejected for a scholarship, despite this he moved back to Paris from Le Havre to help his family. In 1861 he was obliged to serve in the military, he was sent to Algiers, the environment in Africa affected his artistic and personal outlook, after he served, he went back to Paris, a salon accepted 2 of his seascapes, but he felt confined by working in a studio. To escape the Franco-Prussian War in 1870, Monet took refuge in London, producing many scenes such as Westminster Bridge, his wife and son joined him. After the war he returned to France, settling his family in Argenteuil, over the next 6 years he developed his style that we see now. To continue to protest the salon system he and his friends organized their own exhibit in 1874 which was helped in a vacated photography studio.

In 1877, he was living with the Alice Hoschedé and the Hoschedé family, friends and patrons of his work until their family’s company went bankrupt and they were forced to abandon Monet and his family. When Monet’s wife passed in 1880 his work changed focusing on more of the flux of experimental time, Alice Hoschede continued to live with him and later became his second wife. They later looked for a house for them and their children which had a large garden which then inspired most of his paintings. He spent many years traveling throughout 1880-1890 then in 1911 saw the passing of his 2nd wife and son. Many years later in his house in Giverny he passed away in 1926
