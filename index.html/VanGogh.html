About Vincent Van Gogh

Vincent Van Gogh is one of the most recognized artists, his style being easy to spot with its colourful and original ways. some of his most famous paintings being The Starry Night, The Sunflowers, Café Terrace at Night, Almond Blossoms, his many self-portraits, etc. Vincent Van Gogh is a Dutch artist born in 1853, whose parents did not believe in his dream of becoming an artist and discouraged it, in 1881 he moved away and eventually took lessons from his cousin in law who was a celebrated artist, Anton Mauve and grew to love the arts. In December 1883 he moved back to his parents’ house after a while and rented his own studio to be able to create as well as sell his art and then 2 years later, moved into his studio and left his parents’ house.

In 1885, he decided to enroll at the academy of art in Antwerp and left the Netherlands, never to return, which he then decided that the teachings were too traditional for him and he went to Paris, to go to another school. In Paris he discovered and created his style that he is now well known for, after 2 years he began to get tired of the hectic city life that he was met with in Paris and longed for the countryside.

He then made his way to the south of France in 1888, during his time here his mental health took a turn for the worse after the artist he was living with, Paul Gauguin, threated to leave because of their very different artistic lifestyles. He threatened Gauguin with a razor but instead cut his own ear off, the next day he was sent to a mental hospital. During his time there he spent his days creating, some of his most famous artworks came out of his time at the hospital such as the Almond Blossoms, etc. Vincent Van Gogh spent the final months of his life in a small artist’s village in Paris, in 1890, in July of that year he took his own life with a pistol to the chest in a wheat field.
