About Pablo Picasso

Pablo Picasso is one of the artists who revolutionized the use of collage in the way that he did art, his abstract ways of creating still influence the way that art is perceived and created to this day. Some of his most recognized paintings being The Weeping Woman, The Old Guitarist, Girl Before a Mirror, etc. Pablo Picasso, an artist from Spain born in 1881, his father was an artist who earned his living by painting birds and other animals, as well as teaching art classes, from the young age of 7 his father started to teach him how to paint using oil paints. At the age of 13 he attended the art school that his father taught at, which was Spain’s top art academy at the time. During this time of his life, he painted portraits of people such as his sister, overtime his original style started to shine through with his landscapes and portraits.

In 1900 he went to Paris where he met a journalist who took him under their wing, they lived in poverty together, sometimes even forced into burning the artists’ paintings to stay warm. In early 1901 he relocated to Madrid and partnered with a friend to create a magazine called “young art” in which he illustrated the articles and created cartoons which sympathized with the poor.

Throughout his artistic career he went through numerous eras of his artistic style, the blue period, which took place from 1901-1904, during which he tended to focus on using just tones of blue with little use of other colours. The rose period, which lasted 1907-1909, consisted of him using shades of pink and rose colours, this period meant that warmer and happier paintings came out of it than the blue period. There were many more specific eras that he experienced throughout his time as a painter, which were influenced by the time as well as himself. During WWII he stayed in German occupied Paris, enduring harassment he still spent this time creating, paintings and poetry, completing more than 300 works between 1939 and 1959. In 1961, at the age of 79, the artist married his second and last wife. During his last 17 years of life, he produced around 70 portraits of her, he died in 1973 of a heart attack.
