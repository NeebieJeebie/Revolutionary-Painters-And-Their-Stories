About Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo Da Vinci was an Italian painter, engineer, architect, and scientist, he is known for his ability to capture nature, scientific phenomena, and human expressions in many different forms. His unusual approach on gaining knowledge when it comes to theory and practice with every subject from mechanics to anatomy. Some of his most known paintings being The Mona Lisa, The Last Supper, Lady With An Ermine, etc.

Leonardo Da Vinci was born in 1452, he was the son of a lawyer, as a child he was seen as very gifted especially in music and drawing in 1464 he was sent off to pursue a career as an artist and study as an apprentice in the workshop of Andrea del Verrocchio. In 1482 he moved to Milan and he acted as the principal Sforza military and naval engineer, as well as a master painter and sculptor, at the same time. Throughout his time here he focused on his studies and trying to learn the most he could, about art, architecture, etc. as well as the natural world with studies such as zoology.

Around 1500 he worked in Venice, during this time he painted his version of the Leda and the swan story which reflected how he saw the Greek mythology story, which is now lost while the sketches survive. In 1502 he worked in Rome where he was commissioned to sort out the cities canals as well as planning improvements to harbours. By 1503 he was back in Florence working on proposals for a mural in the city council hall, which would depict a battle between the Florence and Milan armies. In 1517 he moved to France, where his skills were appreciated much more, he was involved in the designing stage of the Francis Chateau De Chambord on the Loire River, which was built from 1519-1547. His final painting was in 1515, St. John the Baptist, he focused more on scientific enquiry in his later years. He died at his French home in 1519 and was entombed within the Chapel of Saint Hubert.
